Privacy Policy for Epoch Hover

Last updated: June 30, 2026

Epoch Hover is a Chrome extension that converts Unix epoch timestamps shown in AWS pages into readable dates and times. This policy explains what information the extension handles and how it is used.

Information We Collect

Epoch Hover does not collect, transmit, sell, or share personal information. The extension does not use analytics, advertising, tracking pixels, remote logging, or any third-party data collection service.

Information Processed Locally

When you browse supported AWS pages, the extension reads visible text on the page in your browser to find values that look like Unix timestamps. Timestamp conversion happens locally in your browser. Page content, timestamps, URLs, account details, and browsing activity are not sent to the developer or to any external server.

Stored Settings

Epoch Hover uses Chrome storage only to save your extension preferences, including your selected display timezone and whether pinned tooltips are enabled. These settings may sync through your Chrome account if browser sync is enabled. They are not accessible to the developer.

Permissions

The extension requests the Chrome storage permission to save settings. It runs only on the AWS-related pages listed in the extension manifest so it can detect and convert timestamp text there.

Data Sharing

Because Epoch Hover does not collect or transmit personal information, there is no personal information to share, sell, rent, or transfer.

Security

Epoch Hover is designed to work entirely in your browser. It does not require an account, password, API key, or external service.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated if the extension's behavior changes. Any changes will be reflected by updating this page.

Contact

For questions about this privacy policy, please open an issue at github.com/ilovefood2/EpochExt/issues.
